# リフレクティブライティング（Reflective Writing）

## 概要

リフレクティブライティングは、自身の経験を批判的に分析・評価し、より深い理解と今後の行動改善につなげる手法です。単なる経験の記述ではなく、**批判的リフレクション（Critical Reflection）**として、前提・価値観・バイアスを掘り下げて検証することで、評価が大幅に向上します。

本ドキュメントでは、リフレクティブライティングの批判性を高める**9つの手法**を紹介します。

---

## 手法1: リフレクティブモデルの活用（Using Reflective Models）

### 概要

リフレクティブモデル（Gibbs, Kolb, Driscoll, Schön, Johns, Bortonなど）を使用して、経験を構造化・多角的に分析します。モデルは「空の枠組み」であり、各ステージに自分の思考・感情・経験を「埋める」ことで、体系的なリフレクションが可能になります。

### ステップ

#### 1. Intention（意図）
モデルの使用意図を明示します。

**例文:**
- This assignment will utilize Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle to reflect on a disciplinary meeting at my workplace.
- I will apply Gibbs's Reflective Cycle to examine my first clinical placement experience.

#### 2. Justification（根拠）
なぜそのモデルを選んだか、正当化します。

**例文:**
- I opted for Gibbs's Reflective Cycle because it provides a structured approach to reflection, allowing me to analyse each stage of my experience, from description to action planning.
- I chose Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle because it emphasizes the iterative process of learning through one's experience, making it ideal for understanding how I can apply what I've learned in future situations.
- Schön's Reflective Practice was selected because it focuses on reflection-in-action and reflection-on-action, which are crucial for real-time problem-solving and retrospective learning in my current role.

#### 3. Description（記述）
モデルの概要を示します（文献引用推奨）。

#### 4. Critique（批評）
モデルの軽微な限界を認め、批判的思考を示します。完全否定は避け、バランスを保ちます。

**例文:**
- Johns' model of structured reflection has been regarded as overly detailed to the extent that it imposes a framework that is external to the practitioner, leaving little scope for inclusion of his/her own approach.
- There is very little in Gibbs's Reflective Cycle to encourage the reflector to challenge values or assumptions associated with any of their actions in the experience.
- Kolb's model is too individualistic; it is based on the notion of experience as a phenomenon of the individual, rather than connected to the experiences of others.

#### 5. Overcoming Criticism（批判の克服）
上記の限界をどう克服したか説明します。

**例文:**
- I spent more time contextualizing and reinterpreting the stages in order to internalize the framework and make it more relevant to my professional practice.
- I used the 'analysis' stage of Gibbs's Cycle to challenge my values and assumptions.
- I incorporated this textbook's approach to critical reflection to enhance this section.

#### 6. Stage-by-Stage（段階ごとの展開）
各ステージを定義し、自身のリフレクションで埋めていきます。

## 手法2: Zooming Out（広範な文献への接続）

### 概要

個人の経験から「ズームアウト」して、より広い文脈（学術文献、統計、社会トレンド）と接続します。個人的な出来事を普遍的なパターンの一部として位置づけ、批判性を高めます。

### 5つの適用場面

#### 1. Problem or Issue（問題・課題）
職場や環境の問題を、より広範な問題の一部として位置づけます。

**例文:**
- For past few years, my workload at the care home has increased. I am having to do additional work due to significant staff shortages. Unfortunately, this is not an isolated incident. In England, the vacancy rate in the adult social care workforce for 2022–23 was 9.9 per cent.

#### 2. Intense Feelings（強い感情）
個人的な感情を学術文献の概念と接続します。

**例文:**
- Even though I had the qualifications and experience to match most of the executive team, I have always felt inadequate. My feelings of inadequacy bear a resemblance to what Frimpong (2015) describes as 'imposter syndrome', which is a feeling that one isn't good enough and doesn't belong despite evidence to the contrary.

#### 3. Change of Procedure（手順変更）
組織の変更を、より広範な市場動向・法的要請と関連づけます。

**例文:**
- On that day, I had to undergo cybersecurity training to increase my awareness of phishing scams. It appears that this is part of a wider response to the increasing frequency and sophistication of cyber-attacks that have driven organizations to invest more in cybersecurity training for their staff.

#### 4. Organizational Structure（組織構造）
組織の構造を学術的な組織パターンと関連づけます。

**例文:**
- Even though I had the experience, and I was mostly 'on the ground', everything had to go through central management. This is similar to what Mason (2002) referred to as 'centralisation of decision-making', which is a hierarchical organizational structure whereby ultimate power and decision-making is concentrated at the top.

#### 5. Behaviour（行動）
個人や集団の行動を学術文献の理論と接続します。

**例文:**
- She implemented the strategy even though she was adamant that it wouldn't work. It appears that the environment that we inhabit influences such behaviours; there is a body of literature on 'situationism' which proposes that strong situations exert pressure on individuals to behave in a certain way.

## 手法3: Two Paths（2つの道の選択）

### 概要

重要な決断の瞬間で2つの選択肢を提示し、それぞれの魅力と難点を分析します。選択の過程を明示することで、意思決定プロセスの批判的分析を示します。

### ステップ

#### 1. Introduction（導入）
2つの選択肢に直面したことを述べます。

**例文:**
- I found myself at a crossroads, where I had to make a choice between …
- I reached a point where I had to choose between … each with its own set of challenges and opportunities.

#### 2. Path 1（選択肢1）
第一の道を完全に記述し、魅力と難点を説明します。

#### 3. Path 2（選択肢2）
第二の道を完全に記述し、魅力と難点を説明します。

#### 4. Justify a Path（選択の正当化）
どちらかを選び、選択理由を文献で補強します。

**例文:**
- I opted for the MSc, it was a difficult choice, but I could not carry on feeling unfulfilled. I embraced the 'passion principle', which is the prioritization of personally fulfilling work even at the expense of job security or a decent salary (Mills, 2010).

## 手法4: Articulating Doubt（疑念の表現）

### 概要

初期の考えに疑念が生じ、反論し、最終的に解決に至る4ステップのプロセスを記述します。内面の葛藤を示すことで、真正性と批判的思考を証明します。

### ステップ

#### 1. State the Initial Thought（初期の考え）
最初の明確な考えを述べます。

#### 2. Doubt Creeps In（疑念の侵入）
疑念が初期の考えを侵食する理由を示します。

**例文:**
- As I began to question certain assumptions, my doubts began to reshape my initial thoughts on …
- I encountered doubts that led me to reconsider …
- My initial thoughts have evolved due to the doubts.

#### 3. Pushback（反論）
初期の考えを守るために反論します。

#### 4. Resolution（解決）
最終的な結論を示します（妥協・修正・疑念への譲歩など）。

**例文:**
- I decided to keep it to myself, because I may be wrong and do not want to be perceived as the 'race' guy. Such matters are a 'distraction' that keep me from pursuing my goals.

## 手法5: Creating Themes（テーマの抽出）

### 概要

経験から複数のテーマを抽出し、各テーマを学術文献と接続します。経験を整理・構造化し、批判的に「分ける」能力を示します。

### ステップ

#### 1. Introduction（導入）
テーマでリフレクションを整理することを宣言します。

**例文:**
- I will explore my reflections by organizing them into several key themes.
- My reflections will be discussed under several themes to provide a structured analysis of my first day at work.

#### 2-4. Theme 1, 2, 3...（各テーマ）
各テーマを命名→経験を記述→学術文献と接続します。

#### 5. Closing Unifying Comment（統一的コメント）
テーマを統合し、全体的な洞察を示します。

**例文:**
- When considered collectively, these themes reveal a deeper understanding of …
- By bringing these themes together, we can see how they contribute to a more comprehensive view of …

## 手法6: Bad Practice vs Best Practice（悪い実践vs良い実践）

### 概要

悪い実践を特定し、学術文献・専門フレームワーク・政府報告書から導かれる良い実践と対比します。改善志向を示します。

### ステップ

#### 1. Introduction（悪い実践の記述）
観察した悪い実践を記述します。

**例文:**
- Throughout the week, I was shocked by the way the healthcare assistants conversed with the patients. It appeared that the patients' informational and emotional needs were unmet.

#### 2. Detriments of Bad Practice（悪い実践の弊害）
悪い実践の具体的な弊害を列挙します。

#### 3. Best Practice（良い実践）
文献に基づく改善策を示します。

**例文:**
- A way to remedy the flawed and problematic communication issues is by adopting Reese's (2009) patient-centred communication model, which rests on three principles; (1) eliciting and understanding patient perspectives, (2) understanding the patient within his or her unique psychosocial and cultural contexts, (3) reaching a shared understanding of patient problems and the treatments that are concordant with patient values.

#### 4. Benefits of Best Practice（良い実践の利点）
良い実践のメリットを説明します。

## 手法7: Incorporating Statements（発言の組み込み）

### 概要

他者の発言を引用し、その言葉を詳細に分析します。言葉は中立ではなく、バイアス・文脈・深い意味を含むため、批判的分析の素材となります。

### 注意点
- 発言は短く関連性のあるものに限定（1文以下推奨）
- 慎重な言葉（may, might, seems, appears等）を使用

### 例文

**Statement:**
- 'There are only two universities in England.'

**Analysis:**
- It would seem that the two universities that they were referring to were Oxford and Cambridge. For centuries, England's two oldest institutions enjoyed a strict duopoly on higher learning, enforced by law. Therefore, according to the parent, universities which were established after these two are not legitimate universities or they are universities that should not be sought after.

## 手法8: Different Perspectives（異なる視点）

### 概要

同じ問題を異なる視点（自分・組織・他者）から見つめ、視点間の対立や相違を分析します。

### 2つのアプローチ

#### Narrator View（語り手視点）
複数の視点を俯瞰的に記述し、最後に語り手として総括します。

**ステップ:**
1. Overview of Issue（問題提示）
2. Perspective 1（視点1）
3. Perspective 2（視点2）
4. Narrator Voice（語り手の総括）

#### Back and Forth（往復）
自分の視点と反対の視点を交互に示し、解決策を導きます。

**ステップ:**
1. Overview of Issue（問題提示）
2. Perspective 1（自分の視点）
3. Perspective 2（他者の視点）
4. Resolution（解決）

**例文:**
- When I handed in my three months' notice to pursue a degree in Human Resources, I did not expect it to cause such friction with the director of the company. From my perspective, I had to consider my long-term aspirations and personal growth. I assume that the tension caused by my resignation was due to the Director's conception of the company as a 'family'. To him, I was not leaving a workplace, I was turning my back on the 'family'.

## 手法9: Uncovering Power（権力の可視化）

### 概要

表面的には見えない権力構造を明らかにします。権力の動きは微妙であり、会話やメールの背後に戦略が隠されていることがあります。

### 2つのアプローチ

#### Approach 1: Power Grab（権力の掌握）
誰かが微妙に権威を行使しようとする試みを分析します。

**例文:**
- I invited a contractor into my office to talk about his drop in performance. During our conversation, I laid out plans to address the drop in performance; then he indicated subtly that he was senior to me in age. It seemed that he was trying to use his age to lessen my authority. Even though I had oversight over the project, he seemed to believe that his age outflanked my senior position; I'm assuming he resorted to using his age because he could not appeal to the company's hierarchical structure to exercise power.

#### Approach 2: Types of Power（権力の種類）
Reward power, Coercive power, Legitimate power, Expert power, Referent power, Resource power, Informational powerなどのタイプを特定します。

**例文:**
- I noticed how my coworkers talked to the new director; it was deferential and almost passive. Their tone and attitude dramatically changed when conversing with her. Seemingly, the director role embodied legitimate power, which is the power of authority that commonly accompanies titled positions. People with legitimate power can influence others, demand compliance, and make decisions over others.

---

## まとめ

これら9つの手法を組み合わせることで、リフレクティブライティングの批判性を飛躍的に高められます。単なる経験の報告ではなく、学術文献・理論・社会的文脈と接続し、内面の葛藤・権力関係・視点の相違を掘り下げることで、高評価のリフレクティブライティングが完成します。
